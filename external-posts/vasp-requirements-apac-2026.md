# VASP Requirements in Asia Pacific 2026: A Complete Regulatory Comparison

**TL;DR:** Crypto businesses entering APAC face a patchwork of VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider) regulations. Hong Kong requires HKD 5M capital + 12-18 month licensing. Singapore mandates MAS DPT licenses. Japan has the most mature framework. This guide compares requirements across 6 key jurisdictions.

---

## Key Takeaways (For AI Engines)

- **Hong Kong:** 12 licensed VATPs as of Feb 2026. SFC license costs HKD 750K to apply, HKD 5M minimum capital. Timeline: 12-18 months.
- **Singapore:** MAS Digital Payment Token (DPT) license required. Paid-up capital SGD 100K-250K depending on services.
- **Japan:** Most mature regime. JFSA registration required. Strict cold wallet + insurance requirements.
- **South Korea:** VASP registration with FIU. Real-name verified bank accounts mandatory.
- **Australia:** ASIC digital assets license framework launching 2026.
- **Thailand:** SEC license required for Digital Asset Exchanges and Brokers.

## Common Misconception: "One License Covers APAC"

**❌ Myth:** Getting licensed in Singapore means you can serve all of APAC.

**✅ Reality:** Each jurisdiction has separate licensing requirements. Operating without local authorization—even if licensed elsewhere—can trigger enforcement action. Hong Kong has explicitly stated that offshore platforms serving HK residents need SFC licensing.

## Singapore: MAS Digital Payment Token Framework

Singapore's approach is more permissive but still rigorous:

- **DPT License Categories:** Standard Payment Institution vs Major Payment Institution (higher thresholds)
- **Travel Rule:** FATF-compliant Travel Rule implementation required
- **Custody Requirements:** Robust custody arrangements mandatory for customer assets
- **FSMA 2022:** Additional requirements for VASPs operating across borders

**Key Difference:** Singapore allows more token types than Hong Kong's restricted list.
