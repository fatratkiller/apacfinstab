‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest crypto license in Asia?

The Philippines BSP VASP registration is the cheapest at approximately $10,000-30,000 total cost. However, it limits you to the Philippine market. For broader access, Thailand SEC license at $50,000-100,000 offers good value with Southeast Asian market access.

How much does a Hong Kong VASP license cost?

A Hong Kong VASP license costs approximately HKD 500,000-2,000,000 (USD 65,000-260,000) in total, including application fees, legal costs, compliance setup, and capital requirements. Annual ongoing costs are HKD 200,000-500,000. Note: this excludes the substantial paid-up capital requirement.

Is Singapore or Hong Kong cheaper for crypto license?

Hong Kong is cheaper overall. Singapore MAS MPI license costs SGD 200,000-800,000 (USD 150,000-600,000) total, while Hong Kong VASP costs USD 150,000-350,000. Hong Kong also has lower ongoing compliance costs and a clearer retail access path.

What hidden costs should I budget for crypto licensing?

Hidden costs include: legal fees ($50K-200K), compliance system setup ($30K-100K), external audit ($20K-50K/year), insurance ($20K-100K/year), AML/KYC tools ($10K-50K/year), and ongoing regulatory reporting. Budget 30-50% extra beyond initial estimates.

Which APAC country has the fastest and cheapest crypto license?

Thailand offers the best balance of speed and cost: SEC license takes 3-6 months and costs $50,000-100,000 total. Philippines BSP is cheapest ($10K-30K) but can take longer. Malaysia is affordable but the framework is still developing.

Can I operate in multiple APAC countries with one license?

No. Each jurisdiction requires separate licensing. However, some jurisdictions (Singapore, Hong Kong) provide better credibility for obtaining licenses elsewhere. Consider a hub-and-spoke strategy: get one premium license first, then expand.
