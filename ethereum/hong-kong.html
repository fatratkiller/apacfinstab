ETH Staking ETF: Global First

In January 2026, Hong Kong became the first major financial center to approve an ETH Staking ETF, with ChinaAMC partnering with OSL to launch the product. The ETF allows institutional and retail investors to gain exposure to ETH staking yields (~2.8% APR) through a regulated vehicle.

üîë Key Features of HK ETH Staking ETF

  • In-kind redemption with both ETH and fiat options
  • Staking via licensed custodians (OSL, HashKey)
  • Quarterly yield distribution
  • Full regulatory oversight by SFC

VASP Licensing Framework

Hong Kong's Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) licensing regime under the AMLO requires all crypto exchanges to obtain SFC authorization. As of Q1 2026, licensed platforms can offer ETH trading, custody, and staking services to both retail and institutional clients.

  • Licensed Exchanges: OSL, HashKey, HKVAX
  • Pending Applications: 12+ platforms under review
  • Custody Requirements: 98% cold storage, insurance mandatory
  • Staking Services: Allowed under enhanced disclosure

DeFi Regulatory Approach

The SFC has signaled intent to create a regulatory sandbox for DeFi protocols seeking to operate in Hong Kong. While pure decentralized protocols remain outside direct licensing scope, any protocol with identifiable operators serving HK retail users may need authorization.

  • Uniswap, Aave considering HK-compliant front-ends
  • RWA tokenization via DeFi rails gaining traction
  • Smart contract audit requirements under discussion
  • DAO governance structure recognition pending

2026 Legislative Pipeline

The Financial Services and Treasury Bureau (FSTB) has committed to introducing comprehensive legislation for virtual asset custodians and dealers by end of 2026, expanding beyond exchange-focused VASP rules to cover the full crypto value chain.
