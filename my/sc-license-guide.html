üìù License Requirements

Corporate Requirements

  • Malaysian registered company (Sdn Bhd)
  • Minimum paid-up capital of RM 5 million
  • Physical office in Malaysia
  • Malaysian resident directors required
  • Fit and proper criteria for directors and shareholders
  • No criminal record for key persons

Operational Requirements

  • AML/CFT: Full compliance with AMLA 2001 and Bank Negara Malaysia guidelines
  • Cybersecurity: Robust security framework, penetration testing
  • Technology: SC-compliant trading system with audit trails
  • Customer Protection: Segregated client funds in Malaysian banks
  • Insurance: Professional indemnity and cyber insurance
  • Auditor: SC-approved external auditor

Token Listing Requirements

  • Only SC-approved digital assets may be listed
  • Initial approved tokens: BTC, ETH, XRP, LTC, BCH, ETC
  • New token listing requires SC approval
  • Token must pass SC assessment criteria
‚ö†Ô∏è Token Restrictions: Unlike other APAC jurisdictions, Malaysia restricts trading to SC-approved tokens only. Listing unapproved tokens is a regulatory offense.

üìÑ Application Process

Stage 1: Pre-Application

  1. Engagement with SC (informal consultation)
  2. Prepare business plan and compliance framework
  3. Incorporate Malaysian company
  4. Inject minimum capital

Stage 2: Formal Application

  1. Submit application form to SC
  2. Submit required documents (business plan, compliance manual, IT security assessment)
  3. Pay registration fee (RM 50,000)
  4. SC review and queries (expect multiple rounds)

Stage 3: Conditional Approval

  1. Receive conditional approval from SC
  2. Complete remaining conditions (e.g., system testing, insurance)
  3. SC on-site inspection
  4. Final approval and registration

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreign companies apply for Malaysia DAX license?

Yes, but must establish a Malaysian subsidiary (Sdn Bhd). Foreign ownership is permitted but key management must include Malaysian residents. SC assesses parent company's track record.

What tokens can be traded in Malaysia?

Only SC-approved digital assets. Currently approved: BTC, ETH, XRP, LTC, BCH, ETC. Platforms must apply to SC for each new token listing.

Can Malaysian exchanges offer derivatives?

No. Currently, DAX licenses only permit spot trading of approved digital assets. Derivatives products require separate regulatory approval which has not been granted for crypto.

What happens to unlicensed platforms serving Malaysians?

SC maintains an Investor Alert List of unauthorized entities. Operating without a license is an offense under CMSA 2007 with penalties including fines up to RM 10 million and imprisonment.
